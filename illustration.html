ILLUSTRATION

A cheerful hand-drawn aesthetic with broad appeal

My drawings illustrate how I see the world, which is bright, expressive, and young-at-heart.

It brings me great joy to turn ideas and experiences into images, whether they're my own or a collaboration. If you have a project that you think I'd be a good fit for, use the Contact Form to get in touch!

I am currently available for freelance illustration work.

Talus Rocks

Talus Rocks is journal comic about orienteering in the Pacific Northwest. Orienteering is the sport of running around with a map and compass, hopping logs like Peter Pan, and looking for brightly colored flags. Though the sport is still very niche in the United States, I am grateful to the dedicated local orienteering community for taking me to new places and empowering me to go anywhere. Talus Rocks highlights the gleeful and sometimes character-building moments of exploring the world off-trail.

Yellow Helmet

Yellow Helmet is a journal comic about riding bikes in Seattle, and supports the idea that anyone on a bike is a cyclist.

I'm a former racer of many stripes: road, cyclocross, mountain, Xterra, and track. I cut my teeth in the hills of Walla Walla while racing for Whitman College Cycling Team from 2003-2007. In 2009 I rode self-supported across the country. And one time, I moved out of my apartment using a giant bike trailer.

Now that I live in Seattle, I mostly commute, ride to bakeries and breweries, dream up local bike tours, and hit the trail from time to time. I draw Yellow Helmet to promote and celebrate riding bikes, while occassionally poking fun at the culture's idosyncracies.

Coffee in Bed

Coffee in Bed is a journal comic about life with my fiancé, Kate.

Kate and I have been together since early 2013, when we met online and went on a date in Ballard. We instantly connected and have been constantly playing board games ever since.

Coffee in Bed illustrates the small, sweet or silly moments that happen when it's just us. It's a little less "ha ha" and a lot more "awww."

Washington Trails Association

Washington Trails Association is an incredibly cool organization that I had the great fortune to work for in the summer of 2007 on Mt.Rainier as a Trail Crew Leader.

Today, I am very happy to be one of the contributing illustrators to WTA's Trails Magazine, drawing all sorts of hiking scenarios, both humorous and educational.
