ILLUSTRATION

A cheerful hand-drawn aesthetic with broad appeal

My drawings illustrate how I see the world, which is bright, expressive, and young-at-heart. I share this world through my own cartoons and comics about my life orienteering, cycling, and with my fiancé. I also love to collaborate, and am available for freelance illustration.

As a Marketing Manager for a small company, I have re-branded the entire company and branded dozens of individual events and promotions. Whether you're starting from scratch or going through a rebrand, I can help your business identify its voice and define a distinctive style across multiple mediums.

COMICS & CARTOONS

Talus Rocks

Talus Rocks is journal comic about orienteering in the Pacific Northwest. Orienteering is the sport of running around with a map and compass, hopping logs like Peter Pan, and looking for brightly colored flags. Talus Rocks highlights the gleeful and sometimes character-building moments of exploring the world off-trail.

Winter Trail Series

In a market full of long distance trail runs, the Winter Trail Series offers refreshingly short trail runs that provide road runners a great opportunity to try out trails. With this audience in mind, I designed a friendly hare as a mascot for the series, to convey fun, excitement, and accessibility.

Tuesday Night Trail to Grill

After the winter hare for Winter Trail Series was a huge hit, I designed another mascot to represent the Tuesday Night Trail to Grill series. Similar to the Winter Trail Series, the "TNT2G" series is meant to be an extra-social series that's great for beginners. Using a cute chipmunk in the series branding is inviting to newcomers and communicates the fun atmosphere runners can expect at these events.

Cedar Mountain Nav Race

The navigation audience differs from the trail running audience.Navigators typically don't expect race medals, post-race parties and other fanfare, but are instead excited by the allure of the map and venue.

Designing for Cedar Mountain Nav Race was a balance between capturing the feeling of a more self-reliant outdoor adventure without becoming too serious for itself or becoming too intimidating for newcomers. The tree ring provides a strong image, while color choices and custom pocket knife font provide just the right amount of mild whimsy for this audience without being too cute.
