CASCADE ORIENTEERING CLUB

USER GOALS

Cascade Orienteering Club hosts orienteering events year-round in Washington state. Through a website re-design, Cascade strives to better serve newcomers and communicate more effectively with current members.

NEWCOMERS have a lot of questions and they want to find the answers easily, without deciphering jargon. They want to know how orienteering works, whether they have the skills to do it, decide if it sounds like fun, learn how to do it and what to wear and bring, connect with the club, and choose the appropriate course for their level.

EXPERIENCED ORIENTEERS like efficiency. They want to find events and results as quickly as possible, and one link they can share with friends to introduce them to orienteering.

Current Website

USER FRUSTRATIONS

NEWCOMERS are frustrated by technical jargon and information that assumes you've orienteered before. There is no clear path to newcomer information, and the information that does exist is text-heavy. There is guidance as to what course to sign up for.

EXPERIENCED ORIENTEERS are frustrated by too many clicks to get to the content that they want. There is some content that they are unaware of because it is buried too deep in navigation.

A User Persona

SOLUTION

NEWCOMERS get a large landing splash that is addressed to them and funnels them to a consolidated newcomer section. Content is written in a friendly tone, without jargon, and is broken up by space and images. Event pages contain a side column dedicated to newcomer guidance, without distrupting the site flow of experienced orienteers.

EXPERIENCED ORIENTEERS get access to their most desired content instantly. Navigation is consolidated and organized to require fewer clicks and digging to find it. There is a dedicated newcomer page that they can share with their friends.

USER PERSONA

To create user personas I interview a spectrum of users while they navigate the product. I ask them to perform their common tasks, and get them to speak about their goals and frustrations. I then comb through my notes for common themes to create several representative user personas.

VIEW A SAMPLE

SITE MAP

Great ux design is not just design, but also organization! To create a sitemap, I list everything that the site contains and rearrange them into groups. I then test my organization my creating high-level user flows, which outline the steps various users would typically want to take on the site.

VIEW A SAMPLE

WIREFRAME

This is the moment when the solution becomes visual! First, I begin with quick thumbnail sketches to work out ideas. The design should prioritize user goals. Once a good solution is found, I draw up a wireframe in OmniGraffle.

VIEW A SAMPLE

PROTOTYPE

This is the step that requires humility! I prototype my wireframes by uploading them to inVision, which offers a fast way to make a clickable prototype. I then share it with users and take notes on how well they are able to complete their goal tasks.
